!Albert Einstein (1879–1955) was a German-born theoretical physicist who revolutionized modern science with his theory of relativity and became one of the most recognizable intellectual figures in history. Born in Ulm in the German Empire, he showed an early curiosity for mathematics and physics but struggled with rigid schooling, later studying at the Swiss Federal Polytechnic in Zurich. In 1905, while working as a patent clerk in Bern, he published four groundbreaking papers—often called his “Annus Mirabilis” papers—that explained the photoelectric effect, Brownian motion, special relativity, and introduced the famous equation E=mc², establishing the relationship between mass and energy. His general theory of relativity in 1915 redefined gravity as the curvature of spacetime, and its prediction of light bending around the sun was confirmed in 1919, bringing him international fame. He received the 1921 Nobel Prize in Physics for his explanation of the photoelectric effect, a key contribution to quantum theory.
